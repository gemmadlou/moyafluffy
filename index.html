Meet Moya + Fluffy

June 26th, 2016

I'm Gemma, the human to two lovely, part Bengal cats in SE London. The boy is long haired and fluffy, hence his name...the girl is short haired, named after the spaceship from Farscape, Moya.

We're rehoming the brother and sister. It's been the hardest decision, but as our current circumstances won't allow us, we want them to be rehomed into a loving family, one where they can go outdoors instead of merely watching the world from our balcony. We realised it's unfair to them to keep them as house cats.

Moya is incredibly curious and adventurous, so she definitely needs stimulation. Fluffy is relaxed but loves to be stroked. He will complain if you don't spend some time with him too.

If you are willing to become their new family, contact me here and I'll get in touch.

Gemma x

Their Food

They love food!

They'll wake you up if 8am comes and you're still in bed. Why? Breakfast time, human. They'll camp out in the kitchen come dinner time. Of course, they'll give you butter-wouldn't-melt eyes.

They Both Eat...

  • Tin Cat Food - they're pretty impartial, but love fish
  • They love mackarel, cooked salmon and sardines
  • Cat nip - they go crazy over it
  • They nibble on cat biscuits during the day until dinner time
  • Roast chicken pieces
  • Olive oil...I don't know why

Fluffy Loves...

  • Kettle chips - crisps in general, but loves ready salted or salt and vinegar
  • Peas and carrots, strangely enough
  • Tortilla chips

Moya Loves...

  • Chicken slices. She really really really loves chicken slices.
  • Cheese - we don't give it often, but she likes a little piece
  • She TOLERATES dry cat food.

Their Chilltime

Apart from a few hours in the morning and evening when they're ready to play, they like to chill. On my bed, on the sofa, in front of the fire, watching cartoons if they're on, bird watching on the balcony. They enjoy the chilled life.

Their Needs

As they get older, they will need more age-related care, especially in Fluffy's case.

A Horrible Fall

Fluffy had an accident falling off our balcony which resulted in a dislocated hip. His sister then fell, but somehow was able to survive unscraped. Fluffy is adorable, but unfortunately, not very strong.

I'm sure he experienced a lot of pain as his bones were healing. Even now, if you press his back legs too heavily, he will meow or even scartch. Moya doesn't realise this, so when she plays, he sometimes winces in pain. You will have to stop their play fighting if you hear him cry.

A Long Disappearance

Fluffy's misfortune doesn't stop there. A few years ago, he went missing. 9 months later, we finally received a call. Someone brought him into the vets. He lost half his tail, and what was left, he didn't feel. The rest was surgically removed. Hopefully he'll continue to be just fine, but his balance will not be 100%.

Why Rehome Them?

We Love Them

I would be distraught if they went into a shelter. They're not just cats but are my little furry buddies. So their new home has to be filled with love.

Nomadic Future

I'm looking to live nomadically and/or volunteer more, so it would be better (yet sad for me) for them to live somewhere stable, and permanent.

Staying Together

I want them to go somewhere together. They've grown up together; born in the same litter, and I'm sure they'd be unhappy without each other.

The Great Outdoors

They see the world go by from our balcony on the second floor. I'd love them to experience the great outdoors. That would make me happy the most.
